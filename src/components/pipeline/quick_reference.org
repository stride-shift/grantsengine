#+TITLE: pipeline/ — Quick Reference
#+DATE: 2026-06-22

* Overview
The grant pipeline board and its stage-scoped sibling views. Imports use the =@/=
alias; same-folder siblings stay relative (=./=).

* Components
| Component | File | Purpose |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Pipeline | Pipeline.jsx | Kanban/list board across all stages; filtering, batch ops, gate checks |
| ScoutPanel | ScoutPanel.jsx | AI grant scouting panel — searches, scores, verifies URLs |
| Vetting | Vetting.jsx | Checklist view for =scouted=/=vetting= grants before they enter the pipeline |
| Archive | Archive.jsx | Browser for closed grants (won/lost/deferred/archived) |

* Key Patterns
- *ScoutPanel is a =forwardRef= child of Pipeline*, driven imperatively from the
  board — it is launched from Pipeline, never navigated to on its own.
- *=CLOSED_STAGES= is imported from =@/data/constants=* (single source of truth).
  Pipeline, Archive, and ScoutPanel all consume it — do not redefine locally.
- *Vetting approve → =qualifying=*: a fully-vetted grant advances to the
  =qualifying= stage; rejection sets =archived= and logs the reason.
- *Scout dedup*: scouted results are de-duplicated against existing grants via
  =normaliseFunder= (from =@/utils=) before display.

* Constraints & Gotchas
- Scout market =both= fires two sequential AI calls (SA + global); they are not
  run concurrently.
- Stage gates come from =GATES= in =@/data/constants=; there is no
  =qualifying→drafting= gate (any user may advance that step).
- Pipeline (~1500 lines) is the largest view; ScoutPanel hooks into its state via
  the ref, so changing one often touches the other.
